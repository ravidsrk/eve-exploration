TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) is a connection-oriented transport protocol that provides
reliable, ordered, and error-checked delivery of a stream of bytes between applications. It
establishes a connection using a three-way handshake (SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK), uses sequence numbers and
acknowledgements to guarantee delivery, and applies flow control and congestion control. Unlike UDP,
TCP retransmits lost packets and reorders out-of-order segments.
