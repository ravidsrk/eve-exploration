Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy
into chemical energy. Plants take in carbon dioxide (CO2) from the air through tiny pores called
stomata and absorb water through their roots. Using energy from sunlight captured by the green
pigment chlorophyll, they convert CO2 and water into glucose (a sugar) and release oxygen (O2) as a
byproduct. The overall reaction is: 6 CO2 + 6 H2O + light -> C6H12O6 + 6 O2. Photosynthesis is the
foundation of most food chains and is responsible for the oxygen in Earth's atmosphere.
